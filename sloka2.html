The Bhagavad
GITA

एतन्मे संशयं कृष्ण छेत्तुमर्हस्यशेषत: |
त्वदन्य: संशयस्यास्य छेत्ता न ह्युपपद्यते ||

Explanation of Terms:

एतत् - this
मे - my
संशयम् - doubt
कृष्ण - O Krishna
छेत्तुम् - to remove (or to cut)
अर्हसि - you are worthy (or fit)
अशेषतः - completely (or thoroughly)
त्वदन्यः - other than you
संशयस्य - of this doubt
अस्य - this
छेत्ता - remover (or cutter)
न - not
हि - indeed
उपपद्यते - is possible (or suitable)

Commentary:

At this point in the Bhagavad Gita, Arjuna is in a state of confusion and moral dilemma about fighting in the Kurukshetra war. He has questions about duty (dharma), righteousness, and the consequences of war, leading to deep uncertainty about his actions and decisions.

Arjuna’s Doubt:
The phrase “this doubt of mine” reflects Arjuna’s internal struggle. He acknowledges that he is grappling with profound questions that affect not only his personal decisions but also the larger moral implications of the battle he is about to engage in.

Dependence on Krishna:
By directly addressing Krishna, Arjuna expresses his belief that only Krishna possesses the wisdom and authority to resolve his confusion. The words “you are worthy to remove this doubt” signify his recognition of Krishna’s divine nature and knowledge.

Uniqueness of Krishna’s Role:
Arjuna’s assertion that "no one else is capable of completely dispelling this doubt" underscores the unique role Krishna plays in Arjuna’s life as his charioteer, guide, and divine instructor. This indicates a deeper philosophical truth: that true clarity and understanding often require guidance from a higher power or authority.

Summary:

In this verse, Arjuna openly admits his doubts and confusions regarding his duties and the moral complexities of the war. He expresses that only Krishna has the capability to thoroughly remove his doubts, acknowledging Krishna's unique position as a divine guide. This moment reflects a significant turning point in Arjuna's journey, as it marks his willingness to seek help and guidance from Krishna, which sets the stage for the profound teachings that Krishna imparts in the subsequent verses of the Bhagavad Gita.
Overall, Arjuna’s heartfelt plea in this verse encapsulates a universal theme of seeking clarity and understanding in the face of uncertainty, and it highlights the importance of faith in divine guidance.
