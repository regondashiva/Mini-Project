तत्र तं बुद्धिसंयोगं लभते पौर्वदेहिकम् |
यतते च ततो भूय: संसिद्धौ कुरुनन्दन ||

Explanation of Terms

तत्र - there (in that context or state)
तम् - that
बुद्धिसंयोगं - the union of intelligence (or wisdom)
लभते - attains (or receives)
पौर्वदेहिकम् - from the previous body (or former existence)
यतते - strives (or endeavors)
च - and
ततो - from that (or thereafter)
भूयः - again (or further)
संसिद्धौ - in perfection (or in spiritual attainment)
कुरुनन्दन - O son of the Kurus (a term of endearment for Arjuna)

Commentary

T his verse is part of Krishna's discourse on the journey of the soul and the process of spiritual evolution through repeated lifetimes. He reassures Arjuna that the efforts made in yoga are never wasted and that progress continues from one life to the next.

Union of Intelligence:
The phrase "बुद्धिसंयोगं" refers to the acquisition of wisdom or clarity of understanding that comes from previous spiritual practices. When a yogi is reborn, they carry forward the spiritual knowledge and insights gained in their past lives.

Connection to Past Lives:
The term "पौर्वदेहिकम्" indicates that the spiritual wisdom from earlier incarnations is not lost. Instead, it becomes a part of the individual’s consciousness, enabling them to have a more favorable and advanced spiritual understanding in their current life.

Striving for Perfection:
The phrase "यतते च ततो भूयः" suggests that the individual continues to strive for perfection in their current life, building upon the wisdom they have already attained. This indicates an ongoing effort in their spiritual journey, where past experiences and insights aid their progress.

Encouragement for Spiritual Endeavor:

Krishna uses the term "कुरुनन्दन" (O son of the Kurus) to address Arjuna affectionately. This term of endearment reinforces the encouragement that he is giving, urging Arjuna to understand that his spiritual efforts are supported by the accumulation of wisdom from previous lifetimes.

Summary

In summary, this verse emphasizes that individuals who have practiced yoga and attained some level of spiritual wisdom in previous lives carry that understanding into their current existence. They continue to strive for greater spiritual perfection based on the foundation laid in earlier lives. This process of spiritual evolution is ongoing, and every effort is significant, ensuring that the practitioner is always progressing toward enlightenment. Overall, this verse encapsulates the message of perseverance and hope for those on the spiritual path, reinforcing the idea that every effort made in yoga and spiritual practice leads to greater wisdom and progress in subsequent lifetimes.
