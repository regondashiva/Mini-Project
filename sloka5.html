अथवा योगिनामेव कुले भवति धीमताम् |
एतद्धि दुर्लभतरं लोके जन्म यदीदृशम् ||

Explanation of Terms

अथवा - or
योगिनाम् - of the yogis (or practitioners of yoga)
एव - certainly (or indeed)
कुले - in a family (or lineage)
भवति - takes birth (or is born)
धीमताम् - of the wise (or intelligent)
एतत् - this
हि - indeed (or certainly)
दुर्लभतरं - more difficult to attain (or very rare)
लोके - in the world (or in this life)
जन्म - birth (or existence)
यदीदृशम् - such as this (or of this kind)

Commentary

In this verse, Krishna is continuing his discussion about the destinies of those who practice yoga. He emphasizes the favorable circumstances for rebirth that a practitioner may receive based on their previous efforts in spiritual practices.

Fortunate Rebirth:
The phrase "योगिनामेव कुले" (born in the family of yogis) indicates that a person who has sincerely practiced yoga may be reborn into a family of wise and enlightened individuals. This is considered a significant advantage for their spiritual journey, as such families typically encourage moral and spiritual values.

Value of Wise Lineage:
The term "धीमताम्" refers to the wise and intelligent individuals. Being born into such a family means that the individual will have access to proper guidance and support in their spiritual endeavors, making it easier for them to continue their practice and realize their spiritual potential.

Rarity of This Birth:
The verse concludes with "एतद्धि दुर्लभतरं लोके जन्म" (this birth is indeed very rare in the world). Krishna emphasizes that it is exceptionally rare to be born into an enlightened family, where one can easily cultivate wisdom and spiritual understanding.

Summary

In summary, this verse highlights that individuals who have practiced yoga with sincerity are blessed with the opportunity to be reborn into wise and spiritually advanced families. Such births are rare and provide a supportive environment for further spiritual development. The verse emphasizes the significance of spiritual lineage and the advantages it brings to one's quest for self-realization. Overall, this verse conveys a hopeful message about the fruits of sincere spiritual practice, affirming that such efforts can lead to advantageous rebirths and further opportunities for enlightenment.
