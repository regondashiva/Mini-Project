प्राप्य पुण्यकृतां लोकानुषित्वा शाश्वती: समा: |
शुचीनां श्रीमतां गेहे योगभ्रष्टोऽभिजायते ||

Explanation of Terms

प्राप्य - attaining
पुण्यकृतां - of those who have performed righteous actions
लोकान् - worlds (or realms)
अनुशित्वा - having resided (or lived)
शाश्वती: - eternal (or everlasting)
समाः - periods (or ages)
शुचीनां - of the pure (or virtuous)
श्रीमतां - of the wealthy (or prosperous)
गृहैः - in homes (or households)
योगभ्रष्टः - one who has fallen from yoga (or the path of spiritual practice)
अभिजायते - is born (or takes birth)

Commentary

In this chapter, Krishna is discussing the various aspects of yoga and the journey of the soul. He specifically addresses those who may not fully succeed in their spiritual practices during their lifetime.

Fate of the Yogabhrashta:
Krishna explains that those who have made sincere efforts in their spiritual practices will attain "पुण्यकृतां लोकान" (the worlds of the righteous). This indicates that after death, they will go to higher realms or places of merit where they can benefit from their previous good actions. After enjoying the fruits of their righteous actions, they will be reborn in "शुचीनां श्रीमतां गेहे" (the homes of the pure and prosperous). This suggests that they will have a supportive and conducive environment for continuing their spiritual journey.

Rebirth in Favorable Circumstances:
The phrase "अप्रतिष्ठो" refers to someone who lacks a firm foundation or stability. In the context of the verse, Krishna is highlighting that those who are confused and do not stand firm in their convictions are likely to suffer spiritual and existential crises. "विमूढो" signifies being deluded or misled. It reflects the state of mind that prevents individuals from seeing the truth or making sound decisions.

Eternal Benefits:
The phrase "शाश्वती: समाः" indicates that their good deeds will not go to waste; instead, they will yield benefits across lifetimes. This emphasizes the importance of righteousness and moral living, as well as the continuity of spiritual evolution.

Summary

In summary, this verse reassures that individuals who practice yoga but fail to reach their spiritual goals in this lifetime will not be abandoned. Instead, they will be reborn in auspicious circumstances—specifically, in the families of virtuous and prosperous individuals. The verse highlights the importance of righteous actions and the continuity of the soul's journey, affirming that efforts in spiritual practice are never wasted. Overall, this verse encapsulates the compassionate and hopeful teachings of the Bhagavad Gita, emphasizing the enduring nature of spiritual efforts and the continuity of the soul's journey toward ultimate realization.
